How can a judge’s decision in family court affect my rights as a grandparent?

As you may recall from the section on “grandparents’ rights,” beginning on PG 895, grandparents do not have an automatic legal right to custody of or visitation with their grandchild(ren).

But if you successful join an ONGOING family court case about your grandchild, the judge could:

    Grant you visitation rights if you meet the legal requirements (see PG 919), AND/OR
    Grant a parent or other caregiver custody of your grandchild, and that parent or caregiver then chooses whether or not to let you see the child.[2293]
