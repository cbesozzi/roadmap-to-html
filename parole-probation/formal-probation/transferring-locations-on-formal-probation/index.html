Transferring locations on Formal Probation

I want to transfer my formal probation to another county. can I do that?

I want to transfer my formal probation to another county. can I do that?

It’s possible. The California Superior Court of the county that you were convicted of a crime in has jurisdiction (decision-making power) over county-to-county transfers for probation.[637]

When deciding whether transfer is appropriate, the judge will look at:

Permanency of your residence (whether you actually live in the transfer county permanently);

Local programs available for you in the transfer county; and

Restitution orders and victim issues.[638]

I am on formal probation and want to transfer to another county. How can I do that?

I am on formal probation and want to transfer to another county. How can I do that?

The rules and process for transferring your formal probation to another county are the same as the rules that apply to informal probation.[639] For more information on this process, see PG. 213.

I am on formal probation and want to transfer to another state. How can I do that?

I am on formal probation and want to transfer to another state. How can I do that?

The rules and process for transferring your formal probation to another state are the same as the rules that apply to state parole.[640] For more information on this process, see PG. 196.
