THE BUILDING BLOCKS OF REENTRY:

Getting ID & Other Key Documents, Voting & Civic Participation

The BUILDING BLOCKS OF REENTRY: ID & VOTING CHAPTER explains how to access the building blocks of reentry including: how to get identification (ID) and other key documents, voting rights, and Selective Service registration. ID is proof of who you are — your identity. Government agencies, workplaces, service providers, schools, and other institutions issue ID cards for people who are members. You will want ID and other key documents so that you can participate in all the services that your community has to offer, so that you can legally drive, and so that you can prove who you are. Voting is another building block of reentry, so you will learn about your voting rights in this Chapter. Finally, Selective Service registration for the military is required of most men in the U.S., so you will learn why it is important to reentry in this Chapter.
