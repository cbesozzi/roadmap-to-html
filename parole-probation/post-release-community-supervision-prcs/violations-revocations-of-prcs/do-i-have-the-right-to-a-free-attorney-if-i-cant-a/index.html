Do I have the right to a free attorney if I can’t afford one at a PRCS violation hearing?

Yes, at least in some cases—you have the right to a free court-appointed attorney if you cannot afford your own.[673] You can give up the right to an attorney and a hearing, admit the violation, and accept the proposed punishment,[674] but it’s suggested that you ALWAYS ask for an attorney and hearing.
