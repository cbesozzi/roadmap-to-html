Can a licensing board deny me a license based on my record?

The licensing board can deny your application for a license based on a criminal conviction, but ONLY if your conviction is ‚Äúsubstantially related to the qualifications, functions, or duties of the business or profession.‚Äù[1911] This legal standard means that the type of conduct you were convicted for‚Äîfor example, theft, selling drugs, violence, or fraud‚Äîcreates a high risk of harm, or otherwise prevents you from safely completing the specific tasks and responsibilities of the job that you want a license for. For example, a conviction for drug distribution might be considered ‚Äúsubstantially related‚Äù to a pharmacist license, since pharmacists have access to lots of drugs and are responsibility for safely providing drugs to other people.[1912]
