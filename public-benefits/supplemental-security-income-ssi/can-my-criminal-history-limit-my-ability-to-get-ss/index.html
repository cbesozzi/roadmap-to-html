Can my criminal history limit my ability to get SSI?

Can my criminal history limit my ability to get SSI?

Possibly—but this could be just temporary. You can’t get SSI for any month that you:[1624]

    Are confined in a correctional facility for that full calendar month;
    Have an outstanding arrest warrant because you’re avoiding prosecution or confinement for a felony; or
    Are found to be violating a condition of probation or parole.[1625]
    Here is an example of how this works: If you went to jail on October 30, 2014 until November 2, 2014, you weren’t entitled to SSI benefits for the months of October or November.[1626] This means you can’t collect “back payments” for SSI benefits that you would have received if you never had a violation, warrant, and/or period of confinement as described in the three situations above.[1627] Additionally, your benefits will be paused for up to one year. After that, they will be ended and you will need to re-apply (see PG. 616 below).
    Also, you can’t apply for any Social Security benefits based on a disability that is related to a felony you committed. For example, if you fell while committing a felony and lost your ability to walk as a result, that disability won’t qualify you for SSI.[1628] But you could still apply for SSI based on a disability that has no connection to your felony.
