After I am released to federal parole, when and to whom must I report?

US. Probation Officers function as parole officers and provide supervision to persons released on parole or on mandatory release.[900]

    You will go to an approved residence[901] and report within 3 days (72 hours) to the United States Probation Office shown on your certificate of release.[902]
    You must continue to report to a U.S. Probation Officer in-person as instructed by your officer once released.
    In addition, you must submit a monthly written report, and follow all the conditions of your release.[903]

Constitutional Concerns about detainers

State and local corrections officials frequently violate the 48-hour limitation by continuing to hold individuals beyond the period allowed by regulation, raising constitutional concerns.
