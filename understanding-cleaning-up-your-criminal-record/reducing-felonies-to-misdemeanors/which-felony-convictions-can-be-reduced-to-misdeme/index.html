Which felony convictions can be reduced to misdemeanors?

If you were convicted of a felony, your conviction can be reduced to a misdemeanor if ALL of the following apply:

You were NOT sentenced to state prison[2967] OR county jail instead of state prison under California’s Realignment Act[2968] (in other words, your felony was a “wobbler” offense); AND

You were sentenced to PROBATION.[2969]

NOTE THAT CERTAIN OFFENSES, referred to as “straight felonies,” can NEVER be reduced to misdemeanors. These “straight felonies” can only ever be charged and sentenced as felonies.[2970]
