What important documents will I need to continue my education?

In general, these are the documents you’ll need to have ready as you apply for schools, programs, and financial aid. You may not need all of these, and/or may need others that are not on this list. Check the requirements listed on the school, program, or aid application to be absolutely sure. (NOTE: You can start the process of getting some of these documents while you are still incarcerated):

High school credential and/or transcripts (see PG. 1054)

College credential and/or transcripts (see PG. 1067)

Immunization/Vaccination Records (see PG. 1033)

Letters of Recommendation (see PG. 1075);

List of References (people who can speak positively of you to schools or programs where you apply)[2594]

Social Security Number/Card (see BUILDING BLOCKS OF REENTRY: ID & VOTING CHAPTER, PG. 41)

Authorized birth certificate (see BUILDING BLOCKS OF REENTRY: ID & VOTING CHAPTER, PG. 32)

California state ID or driver license (see BUILDING BLOCKS OF REENTRY: ID & VOTING CHAPTER, on PG. 48)

Criminal record or RAP sheet (see UNDERSTANDING & CLEANING UP YOUR CRIMINAL RECORD CHAPTER, PG. 1132)

For males only: Selective Service registration[2595] (If you were incarcerated during the time when you were supposed to have registered with the Selective Service, you may be able to get a “Status Information Letter” to get around this ban—see BUILDING BLOCKS OF REENTRY: ID & VOTING CHAPTER, PG. 90)

What are “immunization/vaccination records” and why do I need them?

Your immunization record (also called your vaccination record) is a list of all the vaccines (medicines) you received as a child and as an adult that prevent common diseases like polio, chicken pox and tuberculosis. It is required to register for school, for certain jobs, and/or to travel abroad.
